Explore the resident population components of local government areas and the expected number of residents in New South Wales.

How to use: Other tiny cells in other cities that contain the same kind of information will illuminate simultaneously when you move your mouse pointer over a particular small cell. Additionally, a textbox with fundamental yet really helpful information will show up when you hover your cursor over it.

Explain

For the purpose of clearly delineating population growth disparities, the column chart is deemed one of the most appropriate choices. In this chart, each column represents a local government area, with values for Natural Increase, Net Internal Migration, and Net Overseas Migration depicted in corresponding columns. The differentiation between data types is conveyed through the use of distinct colors, facilitating easy identification and analysis. By arranging the columns from top to bottom, the chart provides an overview of the differences between the groups, impacting the population of each city. This aids in elucidating and analyzing the influence of factors such as Natural Increase, Net Internal Migration, and Net Overseas Migration on the population growth of each locality.
